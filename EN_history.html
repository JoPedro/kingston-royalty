DEEJAY & SELECTOR

At the Jamaican parties of the 50's, the Selector picked the instrumentals (riddims) for the Deejay to sing or "toast" over.

A Brief History of Dub

Jamaica and Sound System culture

The trajectory of Dub begins from Sound System culture in Jamaica. One of the most popular leisure activities were sound system parties, that gradually boasted bigger and more potent sound systems

JAMAICAN SOUND SYSTEMS

Produced by the great audio engineers of the country, these systems were state-of-the-art in sound potency and bass generation.

Rivalty between sound system producers

This search for the sound system with the most potent bass created a culture for appreciation of bass tones at those parties, so much so that battles between rival sound systems, that most of the time ended with one of them being destroyed, were common place

Dub Plates

Acetate discs that are discarted copies were played because of their cheaper cost and for commonly having instrumentals on side B.

What was played at those parties

In the 50's, it was common to play imported Rhythm & Blues discs, but in the late 60's, the demand for instrumental versions of Rocksteady and Reggae singles grew, so the Deejays could "toast" over them as accompaniment

KING TUBBY

Audio engineering expert, King Tubby was an important contributor for the Sound System culture of Jamaica, and the pioneer of Dub.

Ascension of a genius

The growing demand for potent, high quality sound systems raised the career of audio engineer King Tubby, and his ingenuity was crucial for the inception of Dub

When he received a dub plate of a Reggae single that accidentally came without any vocals, King Tubby realized the potential of that sonic atmosphere

THE STUDIO AS AN INSTRUMENT

Jamaican producers began to use the recording studio as an instrument. Pictured: Lee "Scratch" Perry

Sonic and musical Revolution

Inspired by the tecniques of the ingenious King Tubby, many other jamaican producers defined the genesis of Dub using the audio mixer table as an instrument, playing instrumentals that most of the time came from the same origin, because they were popular riddims

Their usual tecniques consisted of emphasizing the bass and isolating instruments, applying echos, reverbs and delays to them, and that came to be defining characteristics of the genre.

UNIMAGINABLE IMPACT

The impact of dub in music is understated most of the time, its influence is vast on the musical world. Pictured: Rhythm & Sound, Dub Techno german duo.

Influence on modern music

With the major immigration of jamaican people to the United Kingdom, the sounds of Dub began to spread in the local electronic music scene of the UK, increasing the international popularity of Dub, that was commonly played at local raves and street parties

The revolutionary techniques, the adoration for bass and the philosophy of utilizing the studio as an instrument not only impacted the local electronic music scene of the UK, but popular music as a whole.
